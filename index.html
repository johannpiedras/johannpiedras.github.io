About

  • Now And Beyond

On a 52 degree morning in Los Angeles I was born into an immigrant family living in a cramped 1 bedroom apartment. My father was an apprentice Baker that focused on European breads and cake designs. My mother at the time worked between jobs, from line worker at Boeing to a Mcdonalds cook. When I reached my adolescence I was introduced to my older sister from El Salvador, and with her arrival my family bought a Gateway computer which opened the world to me.

As a kid I was always blabbing away with strange ideas, and held a colorful list of unanswered questions. With the arrival of Yahoo search and Google my curiosity was placated until I started playing online games. Although part of playing was for fun, I began finding methods to maximize efficiency. I calculated hourly earning, projected price shifts of in game items, and eventually started automating elementary game mechanics to save myself time. With the free time saved I would partake in school musicals and events as a lighting designer, or skateboarding with local friends.

School was at the back of my priority list up until my first year at community college. I was making websites as a freelance and studying marketing when a recruiter for a startup gambled on me to manage their SEO efforts. With success from that job experience I switched majors to Computer Programming and eventually transferred to UC Davis to study Applied Statistics. My goals before transferring were to soak up as much information I can from employers and professors so I can start my own SEO consultancy, however statistics really captured my interest so my goals pivoted to creating a Data Science service for small and medium businesses. Now I am here, learning and practicing Data Science from various perspectives.
